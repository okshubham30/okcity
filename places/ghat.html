About Varanasi Ghats

The soul of Kashi lives on its ghats.

Varanasi is famous worldwide for its 84 Ghats built along the western bank of the River Ganga. These ghats serve as places for religious ceremonies, bathing, meditation, cultural events, and cremation rituals. Millions of pilgrims and tourists visit these ghats every year to witness the beauty of sunrise, evening Ganga Aarti, and the timeless traditions of Kashi.

Famous Ghats

Most visited ghats in Varanasi.

Dashashwamedh Ghat

Most famous ghat known for the spectacular evening Ganga Aarti.

Assi Ghat

Popular for sunrise views, yoga sessions and cultural activities.

Manikarnika Ghat

The most sacred cremation ghat believed to grant Moksha.

Harishchandra Ghat

Historic cremation ghat associated with King Harishchandra.

Ganga Aarti Experience

Every evening at Dashashwamedh Ghat, priests perform the world-famous Ganga Aarti using giant lamps, chants, conch shells, and sacred rituals. Thousands of devotees and tourists gather daily to witness this mesmerizing spiritual ceremony.
