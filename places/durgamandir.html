Durga Mata Mandir – Durgakund

About Durga Mata Mandir

Durga Mata Mandir at Durgakund is one of Varanasi’s most sacred temples, dedicated to Goddess Durga. Built in the 18th century by the Queen of Bengal, Rani Bhabani, the temple is famous for its vivid red colour and its distinct North‑Indian (Nagara) architectural style. The structure stands beside the large sacred pond “Durgakund”, enhancing its spiritual aura.

History & Significance

  • Constructed in the 18th century by Rani Bhabani of Natore.
  • Dedicated to Goddess Durga, the symbol of strength and protection.
  • Legend says the idol manifested on its own (Swayambhu).
  • The red stone represents power and devotion.
  • Thousands of devotees visit during Navratri.

Temple Architecture

  • Built in the Nagara style with multiple spires.
  • Constructed from red stone symbolising Shakti.
  • A sacred pond “Durgakund” lies right beside the sanctum.
  • Monkeys wander the complex; locals regard them as protectors of Maa Durga.

Visiting Information

  • Opening Time: 5:00 AM – 12:00 PM & 3:00 PM – 9:00 PM
  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Best Time to Visit: Early morning or during Navratri
  • Photography allowed outside; limited inside the sanctum.

How to Reach

  • Location: Durgakund area, Varanasi
  • By Air: 28 km from Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport
  • By Train: 6 km from Varanasi Junction
  • By Road: Autos and e‑rickshaws available from any part of the city

Nearby Attractions

  • Tulsi Manas Mandir – 0.2 km
  • Sankat Mochan Hanuman Temple – 0.8 km
  • Banaras Hindu University (BHU) Campus – 1.5 km
  • Assi Ghat – 2 km

Travel Tips

  • Best months to visit: October – March.
  • Carry water and wear comfortable shoes; the lanes (Vishwanath Gali) are narrow.
  • Avoid unauthorized guides or touts.
  • Leave footwear at the locker near the entrance.
  • Book any Aarti tickets only from official counters or the temple website.
